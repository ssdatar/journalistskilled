Journalists killed in India

There have been 37 journalist deaths in India since 1992, a 2015 report released by the Committee to Protect Journalists revealed. In most cases, the journalists were murdered in connection with their reporting on crime and politics. Others were killed while reporting in strife-torn regions of the country such as Kashmir and Assam.

Political groups have been the alleged murderers of journalists the most number of times in India - killing 20 journalists. They shot the scribes dead, planted car bombs, or assaulted them with weapons.

The reporters have lost their lives the most in Jammu & Kashmir while covering the separatist movement in the conflict-torn state.

Andhra Pradesh has been the second-most dangerous state for journalists in India, with six deaths in total. Uttar Pradesh is third on this list.
